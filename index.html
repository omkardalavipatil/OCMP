About Our Project

Prediction of Multiclass Classification of Ovarian Cancer Using Deep Learning

Ovarian cancer is one of the most life-threatening cancers, often diagnosed at an advanced stage due to the lack of early symptoms. This project leverages deep learning techniques to improve early detection and classification of ovarian cancer subtypes.

Model & Classification

Our model uses EfficientNet and DenseNet architectures for multiclass classification of ovarian cancer subtypes:

  • High-Grade Serous Carcinoma (HGSC)
  • Clear-Cell Carcinoma (CC)
  • Endometrioid Carcinoma (EC)
  • Mucinous Carcinoma (MC)

Approach

By utilizing histopathology images, our approach enhances diagnostic accuracy through:

  • Data Augmentation
  • Transfer Learning
  • Performance Evaluation (Precision, Recall, F1 Score, AUC-ROC)

Technologies Used: Python, TensorFlow, Keras, OpenCV, Albumentations, Deep Learning (CNNs).

Project Abstract

Ovarian cancer is one of the most prevalent forms of the disease, making early detection critical to reducing mortality rates. This study focuses on the early prediction of ovarian cancer through multiclass classification using deep learning techniques. We aim to compare and evaluate the performance of various deep learning models by assessing metrics such as training accuracy, validation accuracy, training loss, validation loss, precision, recall, F1 score, and ROC-AUC. The findings will help identify the most effective model for early detection, ultimately contributing to better patient outcomes.
